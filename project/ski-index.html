Welcome

On this page, you can explore different mountains and learn a little bit about them. Each mountain receives two grades: one for the overall experience (snow quality, trail variety, scenery, location) and another for the price you will be paying for that experience.

Mountains You Can Explore

Whistler, British Columbia

As the largest ski resort in North America, Whistler is famous for its quality powder, large variety of trails, and stunning views. The Whistler Village and overall atmosphere add to the experience, but lodging and ticket prices are very high.

Experience Grade: A+ | Price Grade: C-

Jackson Hole, Wyoming

Famous for its steep terrain and challenging runs, Jackson Hole is a great place for experienced skiers. The Teton Mountain range is beautiful to look at, but costs are very high for both lodging and dining.

Experience Grade: A | Price Grade: C

Stowe, Vermont

A classic New England ski resort. Stowe offers solid snow, a mix of trail difficulties for all skiers, and a nice village. Lodging and dining prices are somewhat high for the East Coast, but not nearly as pricey as big Western resorts.

Experience Grade: B+ | Price Grade: B

Elk Mountain, Pennsylvania

Known as one of the best destinations in PA, Elk offers well-groomed trails during peak times of the year, but often is a little icy. Quiet with shorter waits for lifts and dining. Costs are much lower than bigger resorts, though amenities are simpler.

Experience Grade: B- | Price Grade: A-
